Troubleshooting a Poorly Designed Website 

What can be fixed in DITI’s “bad example” website?  

  1. Visit the DITI “bad example” website: https://ditibaddesign.wordpress.com 
  2. Using three website components and characteristics from the list in the previous activity (listed below), evaluate the DITI's “bad example” website. Figure out what concrete steps you would take to improve specific problems with the site.
  3. In the discussion board on the next slide, write a few sentences (2-3 per component) explaining your assessment of each component for each site and prepare to discuss your findings with your classmates. 

Website Components/Characteristics

    • Navigation Menu - How clear is it?  How easy is it to use on each page?
    • Design choices and accessibility - Is the site easy to read and look at?
    • Audience and user experience - How well-tailored is the site to meet the needs of its audience?
    • Widgets and buttons - Do they make sense on the site? 
    • Mobile site - Compare the site on your phone vs. your computer
    • Communicating the purpose of the site - Can you tell why this site was created? 
    • Do all the hyperlinks actually work? What about the buttons?
