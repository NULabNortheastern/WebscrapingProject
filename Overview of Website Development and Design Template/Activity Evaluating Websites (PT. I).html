Evaluating Websites for Best Practices 

Your goal is to compare and contrast both websites. As you browse, you are encouraged to consider what worked well, what could be improved, and what you can use as you develop your own website.  Complete this activity with a discussion board post (see next slide). 

  1. Visit both of these websites:
    • Julia Flanders' WordPress site (https://juliaflanders.wordpress.com/ (Links to an external site.))
    • Nikoleta Yiannoutsou's Wix site (https://nyiannoutsou.wixsite.com/profile) (Links to an external site.)
  2. Choose three of the following components/characteristics of the site to critically evaluate for each website.  Take notes for your discussion board post. 
  3. In a discussion board post (see next slide) write a few sentences explaining your assessment of each component for each site.

Website Components/Characteristics

  1. Navigation Menu - How clear is it?  How easy is it to use on each page?
  2. Design choices and accessibility - Is the site easy to read and look at?
  3. Audience and user experience - How well-tailored is the site to meet the needs of its audience?
  4. Widgets and buttons - Do they make sense on the site? 
  5. Mobile site - Compare the site on your phone vs. your computer
  6. Communicating the purpose of the site - Can you tell why this site was created?
