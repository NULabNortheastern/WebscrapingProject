Create a Wireframe

In this activity, you’ll draw out a rough plan for your website while considering some of the website design ‘best practices’ we covered earlier in this module. The purpose of this activity is to jumpstart your thinking about how you envision your website design, who you envision as users (audience), and how you want users to interact with your site.

1. Set a timer for 10 minutes. 

2. Review the “Planning a website” breakdown  

    • Consider: 
      • How many pages do you need?
      • What content will you include on each page? 
      • What is most important for your viewer (images, media,  or text)? This will affect the design of your website. 
    • For wireframing, you should pay special attention to the following components:
      • Look/Appearance of your website 
      • Navigability 
      • User Experience (interactivity) 
      • Functionality (widgets, buttons)

3. Start drawing! 

Use a sheet of paper to sketch out the first pages of your website. As you draw, think about the relationships between each page: how would this affect your navigation? What decisions would you make to improve your user’s experience?
