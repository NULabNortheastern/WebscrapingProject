Why Build a Wireframe? Wireframes Explained. 

Wireframes help to plan and anticipate the important elements on a website, including its: 

  • Design (aesthetic choices i.e., font, typeface, color scheme, etc.)
  • The layout of individual pages
  • Site navigation (menu organization and hierarchy) 
  • Necessary menus and widgets

Wireframes also help web designers anticipate user experience of a website by imagining how different pages should connect to each other and how users might want to navigate through the site. The wireframing process is akin to making a storyboard in order to imagine and visualize a narrative through a series of drawings and illustrations. 

Making a wireframe is a starting point to an iterative process. You’ll consider other aspects of the website planning process (i.e., accessibility concerns) when you’ve got your preliminary site laid out. The DITI encourages you to revisit all of the aforementioned concerns throughout the website design process and continue revising your website as needed. 

You can draw a wireframe by hand or use digital software. Here are some tools to build a wireframe online:
