Wireframe examples sourced from Professor Julia Flanders at Northeastern University; from her slide deck: “Wireframing and Project Design Processes.”

Flowchart-style wireframes center user experience, needs, and projected navigation through a website. Instructions for navigating the website and questions about a site's usability often control the structure of a flowchart in website development processes.

Standard wireframes map the organization of an entire website, and thus include each page of a website and how those pages would connect with each other through hyperlinks or menus. You may also use these wireframes to sketch a layout for each individual webpage as you saw in the previous slide; you can add as little or as much detail as you see fit to your wireframe.
