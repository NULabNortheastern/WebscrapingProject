Big data relies on collecting and categorizing information, and algorithms help analyze that data. As we ask questions about big data and algorithms, we should also ask questions about our own research, particularly when it comes to matters of ethics, representation, and the implicit biases and ideologies located in our collection and analysis processes. Researchers must ask:

  • What information is being collected and from where? To whom does this data belong? 
  • How is it being collected? Do participants know that it is collected, how it will be collected, and how will it be used?
  • How will the data be analyzed? What biases and ideologies may be implicit in this analysis? 
  • Who will this research impact? Who will it benefit? Who will it potentially harm?
