Do white men really make better authors than white women, Black women, and Black men? According to Google, yes, they do! But should we just accept this? 

Information systems like Google as well as data collection, data analysis, and algorithms are not neutral. They can reinforce systemic, political, and cultural biases. They are affected by input data, the way that data is presented, how the data is interpreted by machines, and more. This means we also have the ability to challenge these biases, norms, and forms of discrimination.

If you want to learn more, watch this thought-provoking TED talk where social scientist Safiya Umoja Noble investigates the bias revealed in search engine results and argues why we have to be skeptical of the algorithms we rely upon every day.
