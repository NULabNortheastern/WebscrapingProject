Big data is often privately owned by corporations or governments and is frequently traded and sold between vendors, collectors, advertisers, and the government. And so, our information is the product that is being traded by businesses. For example, we may think we are using the apps and websites for free; however, we are giving the app and website owners our data, which they use to generate advertisement revenue. Have you ever received targeted advertisements? For example, you searched for a new mattress and looked at few reviews on YouTube. Soon your Facebook, YouTube, and other platforms will start showing you ads for mattresses! This happens because companies purchase activity data from YouTube/Instagram/Facebook etc. and they have categorized you as a potential customer.

Because of all this, it is important to consider:

  • How are we being represented online? 
  • How are our data being used? 
  • Who is using our data and for what purposes?
  • How might our data be used in the future?

Watch this brief video from The Guardian, showing the author and broadcaster Timandra Harkness, and think about the following:

  • How are experts and researchers using big data in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic? Have you read any articles that shared results from a big data analysis? What different types of data did the article use? Was it really big data or was it just large data?
  • Are large-scale monitoring and surveillance systems a breach of individuals' privacy? In a pandemic, is the tradeoff between (some level of) privacy and the health/safety of a community/nation worth considering?
