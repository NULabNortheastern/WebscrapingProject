Word Trees

A word tree depicts multiple parallel sequences of words. This is a good way to see patterns in word usage, based on words that appear before and after a term or terms of interest. There are some restrictions in size for this particular tool; however, fewer than 1 million words should work fine.

Step-by-Step Guide for using Word Trees:

Step 1: Go to the Word Trees tool link https://www.jasondavies.com/wordtree/

Step 2: In the 'Paste Text' section, copy the text from the .txt file you want to analyze, and paste it in the space provided.

Step 3: Select or deselect the 'sharable' button based on your need. If selected, it will save your text on the server.

Step 4: Click the 'Generate WordTree' button.

Step 5: Once the word tree is generated, you can type a word or words in the bar at the top, and a new word tree will get generated for the word or words.

Step 6: You can also choose to select the 'reverse tree' tab at the top, and you can see the words that precede the query term. 

Step 7: Review the results and explore more options.

A Few Notes:

  • The default results show the words that appear after the most common "word" (which might be a punctuation character) in your input file, with more frequent combinations appearing in larger text and the subsequent words and word combinations branching off to the right or left. 
  • You can enter any word or words that you want to query in the box at the top; note that this ignores casing (so upper and lowercase versions of words are treated as identical). 
  • You can click within the results to look at particular word combinations in more detail. 
  • You can look in the sections on the right to see both the full input text and the locations within that text where your queries appear.
  • It's often useful to look at the trees for very common words, so consider searching for some of the words from your WordCounter results. 
  • Note that this tool does not ignore punctuation, so you would first need to remove punctuation from your input document if you wanted to do any kind of analysis that focused just on the words.
