An algorithm is essentially instructions given to a computer, typically to solve a problem, perform a computation, or to make a decision. Computers are given input, then they follow the set of rules or the algorithm which leads to the output, or the result.

Coders can create highly complex and sophisticated algorithms, however, the fundaments are quite straightforward. An algorithm is a series of actions or rules that achieve the desired goal. Every person works with elementary algorithms every day. We all follow a set of pre-defined actions or rules to achieve a desired result. For example, think about the specific steps you follow to make coffee. That's also an algorithm! Algorithms are everywhere, and most people don't realize it. They're just a set of rules or steps that get done over and over again (very quickly, if done on a computer).
