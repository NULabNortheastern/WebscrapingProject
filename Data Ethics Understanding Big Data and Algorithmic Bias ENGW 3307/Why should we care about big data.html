Ownership and control

Big data is often privately owned or beyond oversight

Big data is often privately owned by corporations or governments and is frequently traded and sold between vendors, collectors, advertisers, and the government. And so, our information is the product that is being traded by businesses. For example, we may think we are using the apps and websites for free; however, we are giving the app and website owners our data, which they use to generate advertisement revenue. Have you ever received targeted advertisements? For example, you searched for a new mattress and looked at few reviews on YouTube. Soon your Facebook, YouTube, and other platforms will start showing you ads for mattresses! This happens because companies purchase activity data from YouTube/Instagram/Facebook etc. to determine who their target players might be, and they have categorized you as a potential customer.

Bias

Big data can often reproduce results that may harm certain communities and identities.

For example, Amazon’s algorithm for hiring employees in 2018 reflected the historical male domination in tech companies. The algorithm taught itself to interpret any mention of “women” in the new resumes as negative and rejected these applications.
