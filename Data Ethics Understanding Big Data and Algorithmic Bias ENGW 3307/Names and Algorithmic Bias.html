Here is one more example of how translating the social world into a programming language can lead to issues.

Take a look at the text “Falsehoods programmers believe about names” by Patrick McKenzie.

How does a program recognize a “name”?

His text claims that the criteria that programmers insert in order to enable a program to recognize a “name” are informed by programmers’ assumptions and biases. Do you agree?

Here are some examples of the assumptions from the text:

  • People’s names fit within a certain defined amount of space.
  • People’s names do not change.
  • People’s names are written in ASCII.
  • People’s names are written in any single character set.
  • People’s names are case sensitive.
  • People’s names are case insensitive.

Think about if you can think of any examples. You can find some examples here.
