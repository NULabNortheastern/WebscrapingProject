KnightLab Timeline is a user-friendly web browser tool that allows people to create interactive timelines. Each marker on the timeline can present text, images, videos, and soundbites. The goal is to tell a story with these markers, guiding your audience through a temporal narrative. 

Examples of Timelines: 

Whitney Houston: https://timeline.knightlab.com/examples/houston/index.html (Links to an external site.)

Revolutionary User Interfaces: https://timeline.knightlab.com/examples/user-interface/index.html

Components of an effective Timeline:

  • Strong, coherent content
  • A clear series of events and periods (pick stories with an overt chronological narrative!)
  • An overarching theme relating the events to one other
  • Media elements that help illustrate and support the narrative

Questions to Consider

  • What story am I trying to tell? What is the message I want to convey?
  • What are the main events, processes, points, and periods in time that I want to highlight?
  • Who is my audience? 
    • How can I most effectively write for this audience? 
    • What kinds of information does that audience need? 
  • How can I best tell this story? 
    • What kinds of media will I need? (photographs, text, maps, videos, etc.)
    • Which items will require additional detail or context?
    • How can I integrate a balance of text, images, and other media forms?
