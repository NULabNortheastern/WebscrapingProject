Exhibits are a more robust way to provide access to (and curate!) the contents of your archive. Exhibits are composed of pages (which in turn showcase items). Exhibits are composed generally of an initial page that introduces your exhibit and subsequent pages displaying the items from your Omeka database that you wish to highlight and/or relate to each other. 

  • To add an exhibit, click “Add an Exhibit” under the Exhibits tab.
  • From here, you can give the exhibit a title, a slug, a credit line, and a description. You can also apply tags and choose a theme and cover image, and decide what you want the starting page to be.
  • You can add existing pages to your exhibit or create new ones.
  • Even when you are adding an existing page to your exhibit, you can only establish the layout of items on the page through the Exhibits tab.

Exhibit Pages

  • The contents of exhibit pages are organized by blocks
  • Layout options for blocks are file with text, gallery, text, and file. 
  • Once you choose your layout option for a block, you can select the items to include
  • Although some of the layout options display thumbnail images, you are curating the item, not the attached file(s), in your exhibit
