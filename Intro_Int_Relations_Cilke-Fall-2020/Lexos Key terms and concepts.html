The two most important terms to understand when using Lexos are corpus/corpora and stopwords.

The corpus/corpora are the text or texts used for analysis. The corpus is what you will be uploading into Lexos.

Stopwords are referring to the most common words in a language. In English, these would be the, a, she, her it, they, etc. Lexos gives you the option to “keep” or “stop” the stopwords that will appear in the corpus.

Other noteworthy terms:

Lemmas are word replacements. This feature of Lexos is really only important for much older texts with different spellings: Lexos’s stock example is replacing “kyng” with “king."

"Scrubbing" data means removing the messier elements of your data to make it easier for Lexos to read. You can make your corpora entirely lowercase, remove spaces, keep apostrophes, etc.
