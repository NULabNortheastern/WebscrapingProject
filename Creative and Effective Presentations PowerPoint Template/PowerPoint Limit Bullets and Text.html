A key aspect of making a presentation effective is to limit the text on each slide. An effective presentation should have a small amount of text on each slide. The audience should listen to you, not read along!

Look at the Turtles example slide below. The slide with long paragraphs will take the attention of the audience away from you as they try to read the content on the slide. Whereas, the slide with less text conveys the main message while leaving the audience to focus on your speech.

Source: TEDMasterclass  

 

Think about putting the main ideas front-and-center. Additional detail can go in the “Notes” section. To access the “Notes” section for each slide, click “Notes” at the bottom of the PowerPoint screen. That opens up a text box where you can add speaker notes.
