Animations can add flair to your slides. They can help direct the audience's eye and can be a powerful communication tool.  They can also help engage audiences who are facing presentation fatigue. However, the type of animation matters! 

Some types, like Appear, Fade-in, and Fade-out are subtle, while others like Spin or Fly-In can be very busy. Also, using too many of animations can make the presentation look less serious. Animations should only be used if they improve your presentation style and improves communication of highly technical or complex information.

Also, if you use animation you may to experience problems during the presentation. If you are recording your presentation, then it doesn't matter–you can re-record it. But if you are going to present live in front of an audience, then check your presentation in the ‘present’ mode beforehand.

To dive into advanced PowerPoint animations, click on the drop-down menu for "Animations”.

  • There are three types of animations: entrance, emphasis, and exit. Select the text/image that you wish to animate and then select the animation of your choice.
  • You can also use ‘Animation Pane’ to manage the sequence, style, and other properties of animation on each slide.
