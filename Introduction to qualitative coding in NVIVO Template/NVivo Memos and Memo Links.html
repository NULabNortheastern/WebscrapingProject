Memos are more extended write-ups of your analytical insights. More specifically, you can use memos to records your observations, insights, failures, and persisting questions and concerns in greater detail. Memos are a great starting point to begin drafting the preliminary and final versions of your research paper. 

You can think about it like a mid-point between your codes and annotations on the one hand, and the actual text in the paper that you are writing. Because memos are typically more long-form documentation than annotations and other comments, they have some unique features: 

  • They can stand alone, but can also be linked to a particular file, case, or node
  • Memos can be created for specific sources, codes, or as standalone pieces of reflection and documentation

What can you do within a memo?

  • Add and edit text (type directly into the memo)
  • Insert tables, media, and other useful symbols 
  • Insert annotations 
  • Add codes within your memo 

Note: you can import your writing from other note-taking tools (like Evernote) and reference management software (like EndNote, RefWorks, and Zotero). NVivo will create memos automatically whenever you choose to import this data.
