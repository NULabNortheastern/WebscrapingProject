Annotations refer to highlighted portions of text and areas that include comments from the researcher.

When you create an annotation, you should always associate it with a particular code and node. Once you've selected the code/node, that annotation will always appear when you search for all data and observations about the node in question. 

The annotation process is one of the most re-visited processes during qualitative data analysis. Adding annotations can happen at any time in the research process. However, as a beginner you might find yourself adding annotations after you're more familiar with your dataset. Additionally, valuable new codes and nodes might emerge during your annotation process.

In NVivo you can choose to show or hide annotations throughout the research process. As you work, some annotations may no longer be useful to the study while others must be placed at the top of your data hierarchy.
