Cases are the types of research texts in your project; they can be organized based on your selections. “They might represent people, places, events, organizations or other entities that you want to analyze and compare” (“About Cases”, NVivo). For example, if you have several transcripts from different trials, but want to categorize the type of trial for each text, you would use cases to do so.

Cases can be created manually and automatically. The images in the next pages will show you how to create a case manually in NVivo. However, if you have an existing project in NVivo you can generate cases automatically by importing codes from: an existing NVivo project, a survey or existing dataset, or auto-coded source content. If you prefer to automatically create cases, it's important to ensure that your dataset is “clean”--i.e., contains easily readable and uniquely named columns. 

Creating cases helps you:

  • Group related sources
  • Compare sources and various perspectives from interview participants
  • Record demographic information
  • Visualize the project and its data 

Overall, cases help you further isolate your data observations particularly to show shared and differing perspectives across various populations and sites.
