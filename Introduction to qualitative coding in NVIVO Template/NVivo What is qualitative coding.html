In the previous activity, you have practiced a form of qualitative coding. 

Qualitative coding is when you create a list of themes, or a schema, that you want to highlight in a source or set of sources. Then, you go through your sources and highlight particular moments from the text according to those themes.

Here is how coding can look in NVivo - you can see the main text, and how the language is marked with codes and annotations - by highlights and coding stripes. The coded portions of the text mark something significant or important to future research on the text.

  

 

Take another look at the passage you coded in the previous activity and consider the following : 

  • Identify any shared themes or patterns among the language you coded. 
  • Think about ways that displays or color coding might make it easier to see or draw out themes in the language. 
  • What impact does color choice have on differentiating between separate codes? How can color choice unite potentially related codes? 

These are concepts we hope you'll ponder as you work through this module and get to know qualitative coding as a practice.
