Qualitative coding is about interpretation and argument. The nodes you create are themselves arguments; as a researcher, you are choosing what information you value and want to extract (or, interpret) from the transcripts. 

How do I show and interpret my work in NVivo? 

You can take screenshots or save your results from queries to show your work as you’re researching, organizing, annotating, and coding your sources. 

Use this documentation and query results to emphasize your argument. 

  • For example, if you’re interested in understanding food access at Northeastern, you might look at how often your interviewee mentioned food. How is food mentioned? What are the connections between food and first-year experiences?

You can also use NVivo to visualize your data and research in order to boost your arguments and prove your reasoning for data interpretations. The next page will show you how to do so on your system.
