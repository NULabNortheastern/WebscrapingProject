NVivo is a text analysis and research organization software that is available through your Northeastern credentials. NVivo allows you to annotate and code documents using user-created tags (nodes and codes), summarize and visualize these codes, and organize research materials. 

NVivo is particularly helpful for organizing and annotating research materials such as:

  • secondary sources: scholarly articles, newspaper articles, and book chapters 
  • primary sources: any primary sources you may be analyzing such as interview transcripts, texts, or field notes
  • your own notes about your project (you can even compose in NVivo)

As a reminder, tags are the specific words or phrases developed by researchers to garner additional insights about or make connections between different codes (overall themes).

Nodes are summaries/collections of references about a specific theme, case, or relationship that appear across different project items. Nodes are typically developed after codes have been established, and are focused on providing more specific information or examples of codes. In this way, using nodes helps show the frequency with which codes appear across qualitative datasets as well and help researchers gain better insight into which codes are most important for the study.
